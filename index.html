Paprapee Buason

I am a postdoctoral research associate in the Applied Mathematics and Plasma Physics group at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) in Los Alamos, NM, USA, where I work with Dr. Sidhant Misra. Before joining LANL, I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2016 and 2019, respectively, where I was fortunate to work with Dr. José E. Schutt-Ainé and Dr. Peter W. Sauer. I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology in 2023 under the guidance of Dr. Daniel K. Molzahn. During my studies, I spent summers working at Los Alamos National Laboratory, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, and Optivolt Lab—a startup focused on solar cell technology.

My research interests lie in power systems, focusing on developing algorithms and solving advanced optimization problems to enhance the reliability and resiliency of power grids.
